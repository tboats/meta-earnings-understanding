🧠 First Principles Valuation Analysis

1. Beating Revenue (+1.31B)

  • Short-term Boost: Actual Q1 revenue ($56.31B) beat the midpoint ($55.0B) by $1.31B. This translates directly to about $1.1B in additional operating income after taxes in 2026.
  • Reinvestment Option: Represents higher monetization velocity in the Family of Apps segment, indicating that ad impressions (+19% YoY) and ad pricing (+12% YoY) remain extremely strong.

2. Increasing CapEx (+$10B)

  • Direct Cash Flow Deduction: Raising the CapEx guidance midpoint from $125B to $135B removes $10B of free cash flow directly from the firm in 2026.
  • Depreciation Drag: The $10B in capitalized assets is depreciated over subsequent years (assumed 20% per year). This increases annual Cost of Revenue by $2B starting in 2027, depressing operating margins by roughly 0.6% to 0.8% across the 2027-2030 period.
Net Backtest Verdict: The DCF model shows that the **$10B CapEx guidance hike destroys more intrinsic value than the $1.31B Q1 revenue beat adds**. This results in a slight downward valuation adjustment (approx. -1.5% to -2.0% in share price), matching the post-earnings selloff from market participants concerned about the capital intensity of the AI roadmap.
