📉 Case Study: Q1 2026 (The Drop to $611)

To understand how this calculator works, let's look at the mechanics of what happened on April 29, 2026, when Meta reported Q1 results:

The Q1 Release Profile:

  • • Q1 Revenue Actual: $56.31B (a +$1.31B beat over expected $55.0B)
  • • FY 2026 CapEx Guidance: Raised to $125B - $145B (a +$10.0B structural increase at the midpoint)
  • • Market Reaction: The stock dropped from $656 (pre-release) to $611 (day after trading)

Why the Stock Dropped: The First-Principles Math

1. Cash Flow Destruction: Although Meta beat Q1 revenue by +$1.31B (adding ~$1.0B in cash flow), it raised its CapEx run-rate by **$10B/year** structurally. In a DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) model, the long-term cash flow drag of structural CapEx hikes far outweighs a one-off revenue beat. This operational drag pulled the fundamental value down to **$631.03**.

2. Risk Premium Expansion: Investors demanded a higher yield to hold the stock through the aggressive AI spend cycle. This expanded the cost of capital (WACC) from **9.0% to 9.2%**. Adjusting the discount rate to 9.2% pulled the weighted valuation directly to **$609.71**—perfectly aligning with the market's trading price of **~$611**.
